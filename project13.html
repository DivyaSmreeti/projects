Stories about people, research and innovation across the Farm

SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY : Pandemic stress physically aged teens’ brains, a new study finds The brains of adolescents who were assessed after the pandemic shutdowns ended appeared several years older than those of teens who were assessed before the pandemic. Until now, such accelerated changes in “brain age” have only been seen in children experiencing chronic adversity, such as neglect and family dysfunction.
SOCIAL SCIENCES : Equipping kids to defend against science misinformation :A new approach to teaching science can help inoculate against misinformation, Stanford researchers say For starters, the researchers say, students should know how to check the reliability of a source. MEDICINE : Age that kids acquire mobile phones not linked to well-being, study finds :Stanford Medicine researchers did not find a connection between the age children acquired their first cell phone and their sleep patterns, depression symptoms or grades.
